Energy-efficient RF architectures for IoT and wireless systems

Overview

The Internet of Things (IoT) revolution demands wireless devices that can operate for years on tiny batteries. This project tackles the challenge of designing ultra-low-power RF transmitters for short-range wireless systems, achieving sub-10mW operation through innovative power management techniques. By integrating switched-capacitor DC-DC converters with efficient RF architectures, these transmitters extend battery life by 3-5√ó compared to conventional designs.

Key Results: 70% power reduction, 10mW operation, extended battery life

The Challenge

Battery-powered wireless sensors and wearables face stringent power constraints:

  • Limited Energy Budget: Coin cell batteries provide only 200-500 mAh, requiring <10 mW average power for multi-year operation
  • Inefficient Power Conversion: Linear regulators waste 30-50% of battery energy as heat
  • PA Efficiency: Traditional RF power amplifiers achieve only 20-30% efficiency at low output power
  • System Overhead: Supporting circuitry (PLLs, bias generators, I/O) often consumes more power than the PA itself
  • Integration Challenges: Integrating inductors for DC-DC conversion requires large die area or external components

Solution Approach

Developed a co-designed transmitter architecture combining efficient power conversion with optimized RF circuits:

Switched-Capacitor DC-DC Converter

  • Inductor-Free Design: Capacitor-based voltage conversion eliminating need for bulky inductors
  • Multiple Conversion Ratios: Programmable 2:1, 3:2, and 4:3 ratios optimizing efficiency across battery voltage
  • Integrated Power Switches: On-chip PMOS/NMOS switches minimizing parasitic resistance
  • Fast Regulation Loop: Hysteretic control providing rapid response to load transients

Efficient PA Architecture

  • Class-E Topology: Zero-voltage switching reducing switching losses
  • Dynamic Supply Modulation: Envelope tracking adjusting PA supply voltage based on output power
  • Harmonic Tuning: Output matching network presenting optimal impedances at fundamental and harmonics
  • Adaptive Biasing: Class-AB bias point shifting with power level for maximum efficiency

Low-Power Supporting Circuits

  • Fractional-N PLL: Sub-1mW frequency synthesizer with -98 dBc/Hz phase noise
  • Zero-IF Modulator: Direct upconversion architecture eliminating power-hungry IF stages
  • Wake-Up Receiver: 50 ŒºW passive envelope detector enabling duty-cycled operation

Design Challenges Overcome

Converter Ripple Rejection

  • Issue: 10 MHz switching ripple modulating PA output creating spurs
  • Solution: On-chip LDO post-regulator with 40 dB PSRR at 10 MHz

Load Transient Response

  • Issue: PA turn-on causing 50 mV supply droop and EVM degradation
  • Solution: Fast hysteretic control with 2 MHz bandwidth and output hold-up capacitor

Efficiency Across Battery Range

  • Issue: Fixed conversion ratio only efficient at one input voltage
  • Solution: Dynamic ratio selection maintaining >75% efficiency from 1.8V to 3.6V input
