Energy-efficient RF architectures for IoT and wireless systems

Overview

IoT devices demand ultra-low-power RF transmitters to maximize battery life while maintaining reliable wireless connectivity. Conventional transmitter architectures waste power through inefficient supply generation and fixed bias points. This project designed energy-efficient RF architectures with integrated switched-capacitor DC-DC converters achieving 70% power reduction and 10mW operation for short-range wireless systems.

Problem

Battery-powered IoT sensors must operate for years on a single coin cell, yet RF power amplifiers (PAs) are typically the dominant power consumer. Linear regulators waste energy through voltage drops, while separate DC-DC converters add board space and component cost. Power amplifier efficiency degrades significantly at back-off power levels required for energy-efficient protocols like Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) and Zigbee.

Approach

  • Integrated DC-DC Converter: Designed fully-integrated switched-capacitor DC-DC converter on-chip with PA eliminating external passives
  • Dynamic Voltage Scaling: Implemented adaptive supply voltage control tracking output power requirements with <10Œºs switching time
  • Class-E PA Architecture: Used switch-mode Class-E topology achieving >60% peak efficiency at 2.4 GHz
  • Envelope Tracking: Developed simplified envelope tracking for GFSK modulation improving average efficiency by 40%

Key Contributions

  • Single-Chip Integration: First reported fully-integrated transmitter with on-chip DC-DC and PA in standard CMOS process
  • Multi-Standard Support: Architecture supports BLE, Zigbee, and proprietary sub-GHz protocols with software reconfiguration
  • Low Standby Power: <1ŒºW standby power with fast wake-up (<100Œºs) enabling duty-cycled operation
  • Commercial Impact: Design principles adopted in IoT transmitter ICs shipping in >10M devices annually
