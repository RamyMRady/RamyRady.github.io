⚙️ Machine-Learning Control for Hardware Systems

Adaptive algorithms for real-time hardware tuning

Overview

Modern RF and photonic circuits require complex calibration to compensate for process, voltage, and temperature (PVT) variations. Manual tuning is time-consuming and non-scalable. This project developed machine-learning control algorithms that autonomously optimize circuit parameters in real-time, achieving 80% faster calibration with zero manual intervention and robust operation across varying conditions.

Problem

High-performance analog/RF circuits often require tuning dozens of parameters (bias currents, capacitor banks, resonator frequencies) to meet specifications. Traditional lookup tables fail under corner cases, while exhaustive search is prohibitively slow. Process variations across wafer lots mean each device needs individualized calibration, creating manufacturing bottlenecks and field service headaches.

Approach

Closed-loop control: Sense → ML Controller → Actuator → Plant → Feedback

Key Contributions

  • Model-Free Learning: Developed reinforcement learning approach that learns optimal control policies without requiring accurate circuit models
  • Online Adaptation: System continuously updates policy during operation, adapting to aging, temperature drift, and load variations
  • Multi-Objective Optimization: Simultaneously optimizes frequency response, power consumption, and linearity using weighted reward functions
  • Hardware-Efficient Implementation: Quantized neural network inference runs on low-cost microcontroller with <100μs latency
