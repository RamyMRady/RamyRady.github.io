Next-generation 5G/6G communication systems

Overview

Next-generation 5G/6G wireless systems demand wideband mm-wave receivers with high blocker rejection and low power consumption. Pure CMOS solutions struggle with bandwidth and interference handling. This project designed hybrid electronic-photonic receiver architectures achieving >20 GHz bandwidth, >30dB blocker rejection, and 60% efficiency improvement over conventional approaches.

Problem

mm-Wave CMOS receivers face fundamental tradeoffs: wide bandwidth requires high power, while aggressive filtering degrades signal quality. In dense urban 5G deployments with multiple interferers, conventional architectures cannot simultaneously achieve wide bandwidth, high linearity, and low power. Silicon photonics offers superior filtering, but integrating it with RF electronics presents system-level design challenges.

Approach

Hybrid architecture: CMOS RF front-end ‚Üí photonic filtering ‚Üí CMOS baseband processing

Key Contributions

  • Co-Design Methodology: Developed systematic approach for electronic-photonic interface optimization balancing impedance, noise, and bandwidth
  • Wideband Tunable Filtering: Implemented cascaded ring resonator array with >20 GHz tuning range across mm-wave spectrum
  • Low-Power IF Chain: Designed adaptive bias circuits reducing power by 60% while maintaining linearity for high-PAPR 5G signals
  • Multi-Standard Support: Architecture supports both 5G NR and emerging 6G waveforms without hardware reconfiguration
