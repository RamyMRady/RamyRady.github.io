Photonic display systems for Meta Reality Labs

Overview

AR/VR photonic display systems at Meta Reality Labs require precise control of laser diodes and MEMS mirrors. Driver ICs must deliver high-speed modulation while maintaining sub-microsecond stabilization and 99.9% uptime. This project designed CMOS drivers and automatic control loops achieving 35% power reduction and production-grade reliability for next-generation headsets.

Problem

Photonic displays for AR/VR demand extremely stable optical power and precise MEMS positioning, but laser diodes exhibit temperature-dependent drift and MEMS devices require damping control. Conventional open-loop drivers waste power through over-design margins and cannot adapt to aging or thermal variations. Closed-loop solutions must be fast enough to avoid visible flicker (<16ms) while consuming minimal power to preserve battery life.
