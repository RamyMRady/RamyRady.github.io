About

Background

I'm a Hardware Engineer at Apple, where I contribute to cutting-edge RF IC and silicon photonics systems. I completed my PhD in Electrical Engineering at Texas A&M University in April 2024, specializing in RF integrated circuit design and silicon photonics with a focus on intelligent control systems for next-generation communication and sensing platforms.

My approach bridges analog circuit design, photonic system architecture, and machine learning—creating adaptive hardware that optimizes itself in real-time. From chip tape-outs to system-level innovation, I work at the intersection where electronics meets photonics and intelligence.

Professional Journey

My career spans both academic research and industry leadership roles at some of the world's most innovative technology companies. At Apple, I engineer advanced hardware systems that push the boundaries of what's possible in RF IC and silicon photonics integration.

Prior to Apple, I contributed to groundbreaking technology development at Meta Reality Labs, where I developed laser and MEMS driver ICs with automatic stabilization loops for photonic display systems used in AR/VR headsets. At Qualcomm and the Fraunhofer Institute, I designed high-speed optical receivers and energy-efficient RF transmitters that push the boundaries of performance and power efficiency.

This unique combination of academic depth and industry impact enables me to deliver solutions that are both theoretically sound and practically transformative.
